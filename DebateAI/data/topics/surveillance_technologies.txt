Surveillance technologies collect and analyze data about individuals or populations.

Applications include crime prevention, public safety, and national security.

Concerns include erosion of privacy, abuse of power, and chilling effects on free expression.

Surveillance effectiveness depends on data quality, oversight, and appropriate use.

Technological capabilities often advance faster than legal and ethical frameworks.

Transparency and independent oversight are commonly proposed safeguards.

Balancing security benefits against civil liberties remains a central challenge.
